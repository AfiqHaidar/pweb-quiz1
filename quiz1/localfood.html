1. Ketan Bintul


Ketan Bintul is a typical dish from Banten made from glutinous rice and sprinkled with serundeng, a type of roasted grated coconut mixed with spices. It can also be served with goat curry or beef stew.Ketan Bintul can be found in traditional markets or pastry shops. This dish is often served as an appetizer for breaking the fast during the month of Ramadan.

2. Sate Bandeng


Sate Bandeng is a traditional dish native to Banten, Indonesia. Sate Bandeng is made from milkfish (Chanos chanos; Indonesian: ikan Bandeng), where the bones are removed, the flesh is seasoned, and then placed back into the fish skin. Afterward, it is skewered or held with bamboo sticks and grilled over charcoal.

3. Rabeg


Rabeg is a traditional dish that originates from the fusion of Arabian Peninsula and Banten spices. It is made from goat meat with simple seasonings. It has a savory taste and is not overly harmful for those with cholesterol issues, as this typical cuisine from Jawa Serang doesn't use coconut milk in its preparation.

This dish, which is closely associated with goat meat as its main ingredient, is widely available and easily found. It's often served at special occasions such as weddings or circumcision ceremonies. The meat commonly used in preparing Rabeg is goat meat. According to history, Rabeg was a favorite dish of Sultan Maulana of the Sultanate of Banten.

The straightforward preparation method and readily available ingredients make Rabeg easy to prepare and a signature dish of Serang, Banten.
